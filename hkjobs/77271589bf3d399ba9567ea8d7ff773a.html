HSBC Wealth Management leads the Asia-Pacific implementation of our global strategy to deliver world-class solutions to retail and private clients seeking to grow, protect, preserve or transfer their wealth. This involves the development and management of needs-based solutions covering foreign exchange, deposit, investment, insurance and financing products, as well as the provision of suitable market insights and updates, financial planning and advisory services.

We are currently seeking a high calibre professional to join our team as a Digital Acquisition Manager.

Principal Responsibilities

This exciting role is a member of Digital Channel team, focuses on driving customer and product acquisition through digital channel. This role is responsible for supporting the delivery of key digital performance metrics for Hong Kong, leverages on data, marketing technologies, Paid / Own media and conversion rate optimization to drive demand generation and convert leads into sales on public websites, internet banking and mobile banking.  It requires close collaboration with cross-functional team with Marketing, Product owners, Digital platforms, Data Analytics & CRM, Regional Digital Acquisition teams. This role will be focused on Digital Wealth products. 

Key responsibilities would include (but is not limited to):

  • Achieves sales targets
  • Uses data driven insights to improve customer experience and drives step change in key performance metrics
  • Drives digital from an end-to-end from marketing strategy via digital paid (social, programmatic, search engine and partnerships) and own media to bring in leads, and optimize the journey to drive conversions down the digital funnel to achieve sales targets
  • Drives the improvement and automation of reporting and tracking for end to end performances and facilitates data-driven decision making
  • Drives new use case and initiatives with ad-tech / mart-tech solutions to accelerate digital sales growth via a better customer experience

  • This will be achieved by ensuring all actions take account of the likelihood of operational risk occurring. Also by addressing any areas of concern in conjunction with line management and/or the appropriate department

Observation of Internal Controls (Compliance Policy / FIM requirements)

  • The jobholder will also adhere to and be able to demonstrate adherence to internal controls. This will be achieved by adherence to all relevant procedures, keeping appropriate records and, where appropriate, by the timely implementation of internal and external audit points, including issues raised by external regulators

The term ‘compliance’ embraces all relevant financial services laws, rules and codes with which the business has to comply
