Role

Sub-Portfolio Manager in the systematic macro space for a New York-based Cubist portfolio management team led by a senior portfolio manager who has been a PM at the firm for almost 20 years. The team trades futures, equities, and fixed income instruments globally.


Responsibilities

  • Design, research, and manage systematic global macro trading strategies through advanced quantitative financial computer modeling systems.
  • Acquire and research historical and production data needed for building the strategies.
  • Use advanced statistical methods and investment theory to design and program strategies that explicitly forecast risk, return, and trading costs.
  • Oversee automated trade execution and monitor transaction costs to optimize performance.
  • Collaborate with senior PM, sub-portfolio managers, and quantitative researchers to develop and implement new trading strategies.

Requirements

  • Masters or PhD in Math, Statistics, Finance, Computer Science, or other quantitative discipline.
  • In-depth experience in one (or more) of the following: futures, FX, ETF, fixed income products, or crypto.
  • 2-5 years of experience researching, developing, and implementing quantitative global macro strategies for a hedge fund, prop trading firm, or sell side organization.
  • 2-5 years of experience conducting independent alpha research, portfolio construction and optimization, risk management, and post-trade analysis.
  • Recent 2-3 year track record trading a reasonably sized systematic portfolio with a holding period ranging from intraday up to a week.
  • Excellent communication and collaboration skills, including the ability to work effectively with cross-functional teams.
  • Commitment to the highest ethical standards.
