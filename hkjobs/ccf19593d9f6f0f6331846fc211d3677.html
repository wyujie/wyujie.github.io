J.P. Morgan - Corporate & Investment Bank - Securities Services – APAC Regulatory Practice Lead, Executive Director/Vice President – Hong Kong


2023-02-11 - Product - Kwun Tong, Kowloon, Hong Kong

You will lead the Securities Services Regulatory Practice for the Asia Pacific region.  The role forms part of the Securities Services Global Regulatory Practice, and will require significant collaboration on a regional and global level with the immediate team and broader business including the product organisation and the control partners.

You will report into the Global Regulatory Practice Head (based in London), and will be also accountable to the APAC regional business executive.  The mandate includes leading and developing the regional regulatory practice (ensuring alignment to the global operating model), managing regulatory governance model and taking ownership of the regulatory portfolio impacting J.P. Morgan Securities Services and its clients in the APAC region.   You will be also responsible for devising and driving client engagement and regulatory thought leadership strategy evolving it into the key differentiator for J.P. Morgan Securities Services in the region.

 

 Job responsibilities

  • Ownership and evolution of the regional regulatory operating and governance model in line with changing business needs, client demand and the broader risk management and controls considerations, ensuring alignment to the global standards, processes and best practices
  • Monitor in-country and regional regulatory landscape, formulate forward looking regulatory inventory, and proactively assess business and client impacts and perform formal gap analysis
  • Maintain a robust and disciplined controls framework including governance, reporting, business processes to mitigate risks associated with regulatory change management
  • In consultation with Securities Services product and control partners, formulate and propose readiness strategies in order to ensure mandatory compliance with regulatory reforms
  • Anticipate client demand for regulatory service solutions, and in conjunction with product partners formulate and propose how products and services should be adapted to help clients meet their regulatory obligations. Identify and propose opportunities to monetize regulatory service solutions.
  • Represent J.P. Morgan in the local industry discussions, working groups and advocacy efforts – where required – in an effort to ensure proposed legislations are sustainable, practically viable and achieve intended objectives for J.P. Morgan, our clients and broader marketplace
  • Develop and implement a targeted and proactive client engagement strategy on regulatory reforms to include one-on-one meetings, client roundtables, and regulatory client webinars. Develop market leading thought leadership content including regulatory publications and client facing materials, to project J.P. Morgan expertise and our innovative regulatory solutions to clients

Required qualifications, capabilities and skills

  • A background of 5+ years in the financial services industry (securities services, asset management,  wealth management, pensions/insurance, banking) with good working understanding of the APAC and global regulatory policy developments and reforms
  • Strong understanding and practical experience within the Securities Services industry (custody, fund administration, transfer agency, agency securities lending, liquidity services, collateral management, etc) and/or investment management products and services, preferably in a similar function
  • Strong leadership skills, superb communication skills and ability to effectively navigate large matrix organisations
  • Proven track record of end-to-end ownership, excellence in execution and ability to drive results
  • Excellent analytical skills, with exceptional attention to detail
  • Client facing experience, preferably at key stakeholder/decision maker level

Preferred qualifications, capabilities and skills

  • Industry facing experience, preferably through participation in key industry working groups and being actively engaged in industry associations is a bonus
  • Ability to demonstrate inclusion of risk and control parameters in business activities and create effective partnerships with control partners (Legal, Compliance, Business Control)
  • Excellent MS Office skills including Powerpoint etc
