UBS - Credit Risk Analyst, Hedge Funds


2023-01-28 - Risk - Hong Kong, Singapore

Your role

Are you naturally curious and ambitious? Do you have sharp analytic skills? We’re looking for someone like that who can:
• evaluate counterparty credit risk based on analysis of both quantitative and qualitative factors, including fundamental credit analysis, in order to recommend internal ratings, LGD and credit limits
• assess counterparty exposure and evaluate transactional risks associated with various trading activities across Fixed Income, Equities and Foreign Exchange
• prepare counterparty reviews/transactional credit proposals drawing conclusions relating to credit assessment, risk appetite, exposure drivers and business prospects
• interact with counterparties to conduct due diligence and obtain relevant information to complete credit requests and clarify issues that may arise
• participate in negotiations of trading documentation
• day-to-day monitoring of credit exposure against approved limits and parameters
• perform ad hoc credit risk analysis and portfolio deep dive when necessary

Your team

You will be working in the Hedge Fund Credit Risk Control team in Hong Kong aligned with the Investment Bank. The team is responsible for the credit assessment, monitoring, control, and transaction decision approval of UBS's counterparties, which primarily encompass hedge funds, family offices and private equity funds. Our remit includes counterparty credit analysis and due diligence, limit setting, exposure management and ad hoc trade approvals.

Your expertise

• a university degree or international equivalent, preferably in accounting, finance, economics or mathematics
• 2-5 years relevant work experience
• strong analytical, quantitative and problem solving skills
• some knowledge of financial markets, hedge funds, trading products and financial accounting concepts
• a team player with strong interpersonal skills to establish trustworthy relationships with internal peers, business, and senior management
• fluent in English (fluent verbal and strong English writing skills) and Mandarin is desirable
