Millennium - Equity Derivatives Valuations Specialist


2023-02-14 - Core Infrastructure - Hong Kong

Equity Derivatives Valuations Specialist

The core responsibilities of the role include building out internal system infrastructure, with particular focus on the pricing and valuation of equity volatility products (variance/volatility swaps, listed index/cash equity options, OTC options, etc.). The candidate will also be involved in the setup of new products, verification of pricing models, as well as the observation and analysis of P&L.


The candidate will work closely with Trading Groups as well as with wider Core Infrastructure teams across the region and globally, and also with external counterparties and vendors to enable business and improve processes on ongoing basis. Candidate should be able to work in a fast-paced environment and one that is continually changing. The successful candidate will be a self-starter with strong organizational and communication skills.


Principal Responsibilities

  • Valuations – Configure internal systems for the proper valuations of equity derivative products and sourcing/analysis of related market data.
  • Pricing – Responsible for maintaining, monitoring and establishing new procedures and controls to support the intra-day and end of day pricing of these products within the firm. Responsible for publishing and signing off on the firm’s official end of day marks and curves.
  • Primary Coverage – The Middle Office team is the primary point-of-contact for portfolio managers. As a single place that the portfolio managers can go to resolve pricing, position, technology, risk and security setup related issues. The Middle Office will coordinate with the various departments to resolve all intraday issues for the portfolio managers.
  • Initiatives – Analyze processes to enhance operational efficiency & controls, and participate in ad-hoc projects; work with internal stakeholders including global Ops/IT and wider Core Infrastructure teams to implement new systems and workflow. Liaise with brokers, external counterparties and vendors on ongoing basis to resolve issues and improve procedures

Qualifications/Skills Required

  • Bachelor’s Degree
  • Min 5-10 years of relevant operational and valuations experience in financial services
  • Knowledge of Equity Options pricing models, standard volatility configurations and interpolation and extrapolation settings
  • Experience marking and/or product control (IPV) of equity volatility curves and products
  • Knowledge of Options Greek P&L attribution
  • Experience in analyzing and reporting on volatility P&L
  • Knowledge of excel VBA or related programming languages (SQL, MATLAB, R, PYTHON, etc)
  • Knowledge of Bloomberg and Reuters
  • Detail-oriented; demonstrable thoroughness and strong ownership of work
  • Solid organizational skills, ability to multi-task effectively
  • Strong interpersonal and communication skills; ability to work collaboratively with wider teams internally across regions as well as with external counterparties and providers
  • Good team player with a strong willingness to participate and help others
  • Quick learner, highly analytical and strategic thinker; understands financial products and has the ability to grasp new concepts around products and processes quickly
  • Ability to drive and lead process change; able to prioritize in a fast moving, high pressure, constantly changing environment
  • Self-starter, with good sense of urgency
