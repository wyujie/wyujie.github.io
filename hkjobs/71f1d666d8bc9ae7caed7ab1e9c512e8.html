J.P. Morgan - APAC Control Room Lead - Vice President - Hong Kong


2023-02-11 - CCOR Foundation - Hong Kong

APAC Control Room Compliance – Vice President  – JD

As part of Risk Management and Compliance, you are at the centre of keeping JPMorgan Chase strong and resilient. You help the firm grow its business in a responsible way by anticipating new and emerging risks and using your expert judgement to solve real-world challenges that impact our company, customers and communities.  Our culture in Risk Management and Compliance is all about thinking outside the box, challenging the status quo and striving to be best-in-class.

Job Summary:

As a part of the control room function you will work to support the Firm’s Information Barriers in order to prevent the flow of material non-public information (MNPI) from private to public areas. These functions preserve the Information Barriers and facilitate adherence to applicable laws and regulations globally.  The APAC Control Room currently consists of 8 employees.The Global Control Room has offices in London, Paris, New York, San Francisco, Hong Kong, Tokyo and Sydney and operates under a global framework of policies and procedures.

Job Responsibilities:

  • Maintaining and monitoring the firm's information barriers / barrier crossings/ flow of non-public information
  • Reviewing and assessing communications between banking and research
  • Actively engage with senior business personnel to address matters relevant to their line of business
  • Implementing necessary sales, trading and research restrictions and be involved in research clearance
  • Proactively working with other Control Room teams in the US and EMEA to create a cohesive global Control Room team operating and applying consistent policies and procedures..
  • Serve as a subject matter expert for applicable rules, regulations and policies, including material non-public information assessments and conflicts of interests identification.
  • Participate in the Compliance team training efforts and taking responsibility for providing training to various business areas in specific areas of expertise. 

Required Qualifications, Capabilities and Skills:

  • You should have a minimum of 8 years relevant Compliance experience of which 5-6 years should have been in  Control Room experience role. Candidates with Research Compliance or Investment Banking Compliance experience will also be considered. 
  • Attention to detail, good leadership skills and analytical approach to problem solving. Aptitude to identify real issues and know when to escalate
  • Good interpersonal skills, confident personality, highly motivated and organized.
  • Acting as a point of contact as a senior member of the team, particularly to junior staff members.
  • Awareness of regulatory and internal control requirements, particularly in relation to effective information barrier arrangements and management of conflicts of interest.
