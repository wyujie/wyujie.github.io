Compliance is a global function that brings together all aspects of regulatory and financial crime risk management. Acting as risk stewards, and incorporating a range of specialist teams, we work together with the business and leverage advanced technology to help HSBC navigate a complex regulatory landscape and embed a sustainable approach to compliance risk management that supports safe growth and serves customers fairly. Compliance aspires to continuously raise the bar and set leading standards across the industry to help HSBC safeguard its customers and the communities within which it operates.

We are currently seeking a high calibre professional to join our team as an Assistant Manager, External Investigative Reporting .

Principal Responsibilities

  • Conduct effective and timely investigations in accordance with agreed standard reporting requirements and turnaround time
  • Produce a high-quality investigation report including a comprehensive narrative and recommendation as to whether financial crime concern is identified
  • Recommend on filing suspicious activity reports regarding potential money laundering and terrorist financing activity
  • Responsible for escalating investigations to risk owners and risk stewards, including but not limiting to appropriate referral for action
  • Responsible for referring cross border or cross LoB investigations to Regional Investigations for co-ordination where appropriate
  • Conduct risk analysis and provide MI to mitigate identified risks, track recommendations and agreed actions to ensure completion
  • Undertake ad hoc transaction monitoring and investigations projects, reviews and monitoring when required
Requirements

Requirements

  • Graduate in Law, Risk Management, Accounting & Finance, Economics, Business Administration or related discipline is preferred
  • Experience in investigative work
  • Good working knowledge of money laundering and terrorist financing risk within the financial services industry
  • Strong analytical mindset with self-motivation and able to meet tight deadlines
  • Logical thinking and detail-minded with good research skills
  • Strong written communication and presentation skills
  • Good knowledge of application software (MS Word, Excel, PowerPoint) & Chinese Word Processing
  • Prior exposure in banking and finance systems will be highly regarded
  • Great sense of ownership and servicing mindset to ensure efficient and effective customer service processes
  • Certification in a professional association such as the Association of Certified Anti- Money Laundering Specialists (ACAMS) or ICA would be an advantage
