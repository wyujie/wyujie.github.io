Position Overview

Details of the Division and Team:

The Global Client Intelligence Unit (CIU) is responsible for the management of Anti-Financial Crime (AFC)-led correspondent banking relationship reviews. You will be assessing the financial crime and reputational risks these clients pose to Deutsche Bank. The team provides advice for AFC and business areas in relation to our clients’ financial crime compliance program and considers country, reputational and geopolitical related risks of maintaining correspondent banking relationships with banks located across the world.

Your key responsibilities:

  • Leading AFC-led correspondent banking relationship ‘deep dive’ reviews to gain an understanding of the client sector and jurisdiction risk

  • Leading the APAC based CIU team, close engagement with the Global Head of CIU, AFC and the business, and leading onsite visits with the business at our client’s offices or via conference calls

  • Provision of timely updates and escalation of issues where required to Global Head of CIU

  • Supporting the Global Head of CIU in process optimization delivery and on ad hoc initiatives as required

  • Developing and maintaining management information and metrics for relevant stakeholders

Your skills and experience:

You will have:

  • Minimum of 10 years' experience in Financial Crime Compliance gained from working in a financial institution providing an advisory service and conducting high risk client reviews (focused on AFC programs, processes, polices and internal controls) and/or have an Audit background that has covered these above mentioned areas within corporate banking.

  • Strong knowledge of AFC controls and governance frameworks implemented within banks including understanding of typical AML and Sanctions programmes (e.g. good insight into transactional monitoring and sanctions screening processes) and application in line with both local (APAC) and international regulatory requirements.

  • Good knowledge of Anti-Bribery and Corruption policy/programme requirements.

  • Knowledge of SWIFT network and the financial crime risks of processing correspondent bank payments and/or an understanding of correspondent banking.

  • Experience working within a global team in a multinational organisation with proven experience in senior stakeholder management engagement.

  • Experience in managing, developing and motivating teams to deliver excellence.

  • Additional language skills in either Mandarin, Malay or Bahasa, would be highly advantageous.

You will be:

  • High level of initiative, strong work ethic and analytical skills, with excellent attention to detail, plus able to work independently to manage deliverable to timely completion.

  • Able to build constructive partnerships with peers but also able to constructively challenge when required, including experience working with a global team in a multinational environment.

  • Able to work independently and interact with senior management.

Role is required to be performed primarily on-site at the International Commerce Centre.  Relevant health and safety requirements apply.
